## External Resources for Resource Library

These should be listed in the Resource Library page (`resources.html`) as external links.

### Excel and Spreadsheet Skills

1. **W3Schools Excel Tutorial**
   - URL: https://www.w3schools.com/excel/
   - Topics: Formulas, functions, charts, formatting
   - Best for: Beginners who want interactive exercises

2. **Microsoft Learn Excel**
   - URL: https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/training/excel/
   - Topics: Official Microsoft training paths
   - Best for: Learners who want structured, certified learning

### Word and Document Skills

3. **Microsoft Word Support**
   - URL: https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/word
   - Topics: Templates, formatting, mail merge, tables
   - Best for: Quick answers to specific Word questions

### PDF Tools

4. **ilovepdf.com**
   - URL: https://www.ilovepdf.com
   - Tools: PDF to Word, Word to PDF, Merge, Split, Compress, OCR, Convert
   - Best for: Free, fast PDF conversions without installing software

5. **smallpdf.com**
   - URL: https://smallpdf.com
   - Tools: PDF Editor, Converter, Compressor, Merger, Splitter
   - Best for: Simple interface and quick tasks on any device

### Additional Workplace Skills

6. **Microsoft 365 Training Center**
   - URL: https://support.microsoft.com/training
   - Topics: Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook, Teams
   - Best for: Comprehensive office skills training

7. **GCF LearnFree — Microsoft Office**
   - URL: https://edu.gcfglobal.org/en/topics/office/
   - Topics: Free tutorials on Word, Excel, and more
   - Best for: Self-paced learners who prefer video and text combined

8. **Excel Easy**
   - URL: https://www.excel-easy.com
   - Topics: Formulas, functions, data analysis, charts
   - Best for: Beginners who want simple, clear Excel explanations

## How to Use These Resources

### For Learners

1. **Download templates** before starting a lesson. Open them alongside the lesson content to see how formulas and formatting work in a real file.
2. **Print PDF guides** and keep them next to your computer for quick reference while working.
3. **Use practice files** to complete the exercises. If you make a mistake, download a fresh copy and try again.
4. **Visit external resources** when you want to go deeper on a topic. The W3Schools Excel tutorial is excellent for reviewing formulas.

### For Instructors / Future Admins

1. **Templates** can be pre-loaded with company-specific data (logo, address, currency) before distributing to learners.
2. **PDF guides** can be updated with screenshots from your specific version of Microsoft Office.
3. **External links** should be checked quarterly to ensure they are still active. If a link breaks, replace it with an alternative from the list above.

---

## File Size and Accessibility Notes

- All templates are designed to be lightweight (under 100KB) for easy download on slow connections.
- All PDF guides should be created with accessible fonts (Arial, Calibri, or similar) for screen readers.
- All Excel templates use standard formulas compatible with Excel 2010 and newer, as well as Google Sheets.
- All Word templates use standard features compatible with Word 2010 and newer.
